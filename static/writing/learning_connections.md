# Understanding in learning

My idea of understanding arises from learning math; there is a difference between memorization and comprehension of lets say formulas that drives my definition of understanding. To me, it is a complete (intuitive and detailed) perception of an idea that allows for extrapolation and connection building. It has made my memory of the idea much better, and allowed me to be autonomous with my application of the idea.

It‚Äôs a big principle during my own learning‚Äîa consistent goal I have had for the classes I take is to understand the class content and any other important ideas well. This has extrapolated to my teaching as well. For example, when teaching my friend Precalculus, I would explain something but always ask him to demonstrate the technique through independent work; I would refuse to help him when I was trying to gauge his understanding of a certain topic.[^3]

# Gaining understanding

The ways that I see myself gaining this understanding is through new perspectives, connections. Going back to learning math, understanding Calculus deeply required a lot of new thinking and connections.[^4]

The new thinking included concepts like differentials, limits, smoothness, fundamental attributes of rates of change, accumulation convergence, and approximations. Without opening my scope of experience to these ideas, I would not have been able to understand Calculus well, because they are so fundamental to what Calculus is all about. 

Not only that, I needed to connect them. 

* differentials and limits come up in derivatives and integrals  
* rates of change, approximations are so present in real life  
* approximations are used in derivatives, integrals, limits, series,   
* convergence is basically a limit  
* real life systems have convergence conditions as well  
* approximations are expected to converge to the correct answer  
* smoothness is defined by limits  
* smoothness is just keep your pen on the paper (asymptotes, holes, jumps)  
* fundamental theorem of calculus connects rates of change and accumulation (integrals)  
* limits are really related to infinity (‚Äúwhat will happen if i just keep dividing 1 by 2 over and over again?‚Äù)  
* accumulation can be seen as pouring water into a tub or moving with a certain speed

By broadening my scope of ideas and connecting them (between themselves and to things I previously understood), I understood Calculus. From there, I was able to apply it to many different problems (cs, physics, bio, chem, other math), connect it to new things I learn, start understanding things that require Calculus as a prerequisite, use it as a way to think about life and many things that happen in life, and talk about it with others\!

Another cool application of perspective is the fact that framing is powerful. I can choose to frame a certain project as something with a lot of potential and possible correctness instead of a boring and repetitive application problem that I have gotten incorrect many times in the past,[^7] which will directly increase my passion for and willingness to work on it (optimism). Similarly, in the mornings, I can tell myself that bad things might happen throughout the day, and I should be prepared for them, which will make me more ready to ground myself when they do happen (pessimism). Both are powerful techniques.

Furthermore, since new perspectives are powerful, a source of productivity for teams is revealed: more people means more perspectives.

# good source of perspective and connection: people, new experiences

## New experiences

Experiencing new things gives you more perspective and lets you connect the experience / understanding gained to previous experiences. It‚Äôs a great way to learn.

They also get your brain active, because humans were built to constantly adapt to new environments. For example, when I went to [Math In the Mountains](https://www.moonflowers.xyz/writing/math_in_the_mountains), I was really inspired by the people and environment there, which prompted me to think about and try a lot of new things like a camp that I could make, what to do with friends at home, updates to my website, reflection, gratitude, addiction (I wrote it while at camp), cool math puzzles, and teaching strategies. At an internship with [Orbit Engineering](https://www.linkedin.com/company/themagicfactory/), I thought about many more: intentionality driven work, giving feedback, improvement, fulfillment, discomfort, more reflection (wrote it), connection (this).

Examples of new experiences that were cool to understand:

* wearing glasses, might switch to contacts \-\> understand how people with glasses feel  
* coffeehouse was an extremely unpleasant experience but taught me so much more than something pleasant

Engaging in new experiences can also help you discover new tools, people, or routines that really benefit you; an example being (and this is definitely not the most important thing exploration has given me, it's just a really clear example) when I realized there is a route from my house to my closest caltrain station that halves the time of commute.
