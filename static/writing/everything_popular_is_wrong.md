While you may think this isn't true, it is. Think about Albert Einstein. He's pretty popular, isn't he? He was [wrong](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/what-einstein-got-wrong/).

Jokes aside, the saying has a lot of merit.

We are fundamentally social creatures, so a craving for connection is innate. **The most basic aspect of connection, it turns out, is similarity**. Heck, we even instinctively and unconsciously [mirror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mirroring) each other!

A way that this can be manifested poorly is to inherit your actions, beliefs, and dreams from whoever is loudest (*cough cough* *social media*). To give your undivided attention to whoever is funniest or gives you the most dopamine. To believe whoever is most extreme and cool. For a while, running on borrowed beliefs actually does feel like motivation. But it stops feeling like that when you've been running in circles for a month.

The situation is much much worse for some than others. Young people whose filters haven't been built yet, people in high-density influence environments like the bay area, anyone using noisy social media platforms like LinkedIn, Instagram, or X. At the limit, we get things like psychosis and depression.

The reason I'm writing about this is because for a really long time I had this problem. Now thinking, I can recall a mentor literally saying this verbatim to me: "You need more of a belief filter." I can remember talking to people more senior to me and instead of listening, morphing. I talk about how similar we are, run their narrative for a while as if it were mine. Say "we" all the time. I caught myself doing it for a few months with a friend's worldview almost whole, AI-hype included. More about it [here](https://moonflowers.xyz/writing/on_connection#notably-very-much-a-people-pleaser-connection-addict)!

What has been most useful for me has been confidence. I [previously wrote about it](https://www.moonflowers.xyz/writing/how_confidence_changed_my_life), but I've since then realized that there is much more to it than just an intellectual understanding. My current understanding is that there are three components to a lack of confidence:

- Lack of understanding of confidence (why care? What does it look like?)
- Insecurities / Trauma
- Lack of experiences being confident (viscerally feeling the power)

After that, a filter is more naturally created.

["Nobody is going to teach you to think for yourself. A large fraction of what people around you believe is mistaken. Internalize this and practice coming up with your own worldview. The correlation between it and those around you shouldn't be too strong unless you think you were especially lucky in your initial conditions."](https://patrickcollison.com/advice)

"If you're in the US and go to a good school, there are a lot of forces that will push you towards following traintracks laid by others rather than charting a course yourself. Make sure that the things you're pursuing are weird things that you want to pursue, not whatever the standard path is. Heuristic: do your friends at school think your path is a bit strange? If not, maybe it's too normal." ↑ same source

["When you're surrounded by so many different people who don't understand you, you need to maintain your own individualism."](https://notes.ekzhang.com/reflections/school-home)
