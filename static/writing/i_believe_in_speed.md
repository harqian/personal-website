My name is Harrison and I believe in speed.

Though I don't do cross country, I like to run fast.  
Chasing after a ball across the tennis court,  
Sprinting across the frisbee field and feeling the wind on my face,  
Just like the wind on my face when biking down a hill,  
Or the wind on my hand when I stick it out a car window.  
Oh, the first derivative of position  
The bringer of progress.

I first realized speed was important in non-sports settings over the summer when Steven (my boss), without thinking, paid five hundred dollars for an app we were only maybe going to use. Then I saw it again: "how long do you think it will take to become an expert on REM sleep? A day?" he said.

Now I notice it in my own life. I've repeatedly told people that "time does not in fact fly" because I like to do so many things in a day. Anything I do, I rush—homework, research, conversations. Things I now say pretty often: "What time is it?"; "Damn I just wasted thirty minutes"; "Time is worth more than money."

It brings great joys:
- More time
- More intentionality

The flip side of the coin:
- Biking by things too fast to not be able to appreciate them enough; e.g. not appreciating life / missing important things / tunnel vision
- Biking too fast and crashing / hitting something; e.g. burning out

Despite these dangers, I persist in my belief.

I'm Harrison and this I believe.
